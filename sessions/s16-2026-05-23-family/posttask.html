Part 1 — Register Shift Practice: Spoken → Formal Written (Del español hablado al español escrito formal)
Spoken / Informal (hablado) Formal Written (escrito formal)
Mi abuela es muy simpática.
Mi primo es tímido pero inteligente.
El suegro de Jorge es estricto y justo.
Mi hermana es extrovertida y generosa.

For formal written Spanish, consider: replacing short spoken phrases with fuller formal verb phrases; using se caracteriza por, se distingue por; adding a formal connector like Asimismo, or Cabe señalar que.

Part 2 — Formal Email Writing Prompt (Tarea de escritura formal)
Situation: During today's activity, you and your partner worked through a complication — you needed to agree on exactly two adjectives to describe el suegro (Papá Roberto), because each of you had different information. After negotiating, your pair reached a consensus description. Now you will write a formal email to a pen pal from a partner school, describing the family you and your partner constructed together — using your agreed-upon language, including the negotiated description of el suegro.

Write a formal email (minimum 6 sentences) to your pen pal, introducing the family and describing at least four family members using ser + descriptive adjectives. Include the description of el suegro that you and your partner agreed on in Paso 4.

  • Use a formal salutation (e.g., Estimado/a [nombre]:)
  • Describe at least four family members using Mi/Su [familiar] es [adjetivo].
  • Include the consensus description: El suegro de Jorge es ___ y ___.
  • Use at least one of the formal written phrases from Part 1's register shift table.
  • Close with a formal closing (e.g., Atentamente, [nombre])
Self-Correction Checklist — Before you submit, check:
  • I used ser (not estar) for all permanent descriptions (es alto/a, es simpático/a).
  • My adjectives agree with the gender of each family member (e.g., la hermana es generosa, not generoso).
  • I included the negotiated description of el suegro that my partner and I agreed on in Paso 4.
  • I used a formal salutation and a formal closing phrase in my email.
  • I included at least one formal written phrase from the register shift table in Part 1.
Connecting to Real Language Use / Conexión con el mundo real

Describing family members accurately is one of the first things you need to do when writing formally in Spanish — whether in an email to a pen pal, a letter to a Spanish-speaking organization, or a school application for a study-abroad program. The ability to use ser + adjective with correct gender agreement is foundational to all formal written Spanish description. Keep your email today as a model for future formal writing tasks.
