Post-Task Teacher Key — Describe Members of the Family (Session s16 · Round 3)

DO NOT DISTRIBUTE
Scenario Anchor — Round 3 Context
Genre: Formal email (correo electrónico formal)
Audience: Pen pal at a partner school (formal written register)
Complication echo: Students negotiated two adjectives for el suegro (Papá Roberto) — the writing prompt asks them to include this consensus description in their email, keeping the complication's resolution alive in the post-task output.
Outcome echo: Students present the complete family portrait they assembled in Paso 5, translating it into formal written Spanish.
Stakes line: The email functions as a first introduction of a Spanish-speaking family to a peer audience — tone and accuracy matter for real communicative credibility.
Register-shift pattern: informal_to_formal_verb_phrase — spoken/informal verb phrases and adjective chains are replaced with formal written equivalents in Part 1 of the student artifact.
Part 1 — Register Shift Table: Sample Formal Responses
Accept any formal transformation that replaces the spoken phrase with a fuller formal written construction. Samples below; multiple correct responses exist.
Spoken/Informal Sample Formal Written Response
Mi abuela es muy simpática. Mi abuela se caracteriza por su trato amable y su disposición cordial hacia los demás.
Mi primo es tímido pero inteligente. Mi primo se distingue por su reserva natural, aunque posee una inteligencia notable.
El suegro de Jorge es estricto y justo. El suegro de Jorge es una persona de carácter firme y criterio justo, cualidades que lo definen como figura de autoridad familiar.
Mi hermana es extrovertida y generosa. Mi hermana se destaca por su carácter extrovertido y su notable generosidad con quienes la rodean.
Minimum acceptable standard: The student replaces the simple ser + adjective construction with a formal verb phrase (se caracteriza por, se distingue por, se destaca por) or a relative clause structure. A direct synonym swap (e.g., muy simpática → sumamente amable) is partial credit only — the criterion targets verb-phrase restructuring, not vocabulary substitution.
Part 2 — Model Formal Email Response

Estimada Sofía:

Me alegra presentarte a la familia que conocí en nuestra actividad de hoy. La familia es grande y unida, y cada miembro se distingue por sus propias cualidades.

El abuelo Carlos es alto y serio; se caracteriza por su presencia tranquila y su respeto a la tradición familiar. La madre, Elena, es simpática y trabajadora — es el corazón de la familia. La hermana Sofía se destaca por su carácter extrovertido y su notable generosidad.

En cuanto al suegro de Jorge, mi compañero y yo acordamos que es estricto y justo — una persona de criterio firme que todos respetan.

Espero que puedas conocer a esta familia tan interesante. Atentamente,

[Nombre del estudiante]

Key features to look for: formal salutation; at least four ser + adjective descriptions; inclusion of the negotiated suegro description; at least one register-shift phrase from Part 1; formal closing.
Quick Scoring Rubric (4-point holistic)
ScoreDescriptor
4 Formal salutation + closing; ≥4 family members described with ser + adjective; negotiated suegro description included; ≥1 register-shift phrase from Part 1; gender agreement correct throughout.
3 Formal structure present; 3–4 family members described; suegro description included; minor gender-agreement errors (1–2).
2 Informal or missing salutation; ≤2 family members described; suegro description missing OR register-shift phrase absent; several gender-agreement errors.
1 Minimal Spanish; no formal structure; descriptions mostly absent or incomprehensible.
Grammar Notes — Common Errors to Anticipate
ser vs. estar: Students may write está simpática. In formal email describing permanent character traits, es is required. Note it on the paper and address in whole-class feedback.
Gender agreement: Watch el abuelo es seria (wrong) vs. el abuelo es serio (right). Invariable adjectives (inteligente, grande) are a common sticking point.
Register: A student who only writes Mi hermana es muy generosa (spoken register unchanged) has not completed the register shift. Use the Part 1 table as a reference during conferencing.
Coherence with Paso 4: If a student's email does not include the negotiated description of el suegro, ask them to check the prompt requirements and revise. The connection to the negotiated outcome is the post-task's pedagogical core.
